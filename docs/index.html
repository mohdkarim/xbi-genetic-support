Methodology

Study Design

We analyzed the complete set of 126 holdings in the SPDR S&P Biotech ETF (XBI) as of January 2020, sourced from the fund's SEC N-PORT filing. Companies were classified as genetically-supported (GS) or non-GS based on their clinical pipeline's overlap with genetic evidence from the Open Targets Platform. Equal-weight portfolio returns were measured from January 2, 2020 to February 21, 2026.

Genetic Support Classification

A company is classified as genetically-supported (GS) if at least 50% of its scoreable gene–disease pairs have an Open Targets genetic association score greater than 0.10. Each company's clinical pipeline was mapped to gene–disease pairs via ClinicalTrials.gov data linked to ChEMBL drug–target annotations.

Open Targets Scoring

Genetic association scores were obtained using a two-tier approach to minimize look-ahead bias:

  • Tier 1: Open Targets release 20.02 (February 2020) — the most recent data available at the study start date, ensuring zero look-ahead bias.
  • Tier 2: Current Open Targets Platform API — used only when gene–disease pairs were absent from the 20.02 release. Results flagged with evidence dating metadata.

A conservative Mendelian-only sub-analysis restricts to companies where genetic evidence comes exclusively from Mendelian disease sources (ClinGen, Genomics England, OMIM), which are time-invariant and carry zero look-ahead risk.

Data Sources

  • Holdings: XBI ETF N-PORT filing (December 31, 2019)
  • Pipeline: ClinicalTrials.gov + ChEMBL drug-target annotations
  • Genetic data: Open Targets Platform (release 20.02 + current API)
  • Stock returns: Yahoo Finance, with manual research for delisted, acquired, and bankrupt companies

Statistical Methods

Portfolio returns are computed as the simple (equal-weight) mean of individual company total returns over the study period. 95% confidence intervals are estimated via the percentile bootstrap method with 2,000 iterations (seed = 42). Alpha is defined as the difference in mean returns between GS and non-GS portfolios.

Limitations

  • Equal-weight portfolios do not reflect actual XBI ETF weighting (modified equal-weight with quarterly rebalancing).
  • Tier 2 OT API scores may incorporate data not available in January 2020, introducing potential look-ahead bias for non-Mendelian sources.
  • Company-level GS classification aggregates across all pipeline programs; individual drug outcomes may vary.
  • No transaction costs, slippage, or rebalancing are modeled.
